The book in brief

One woman, one guesthouse and a society in transformation

Hanna's House is the first cohesive account of Johanna “Hanna” Charlotta Lindh (1839–1916), the woman behind Pensionat Granparken.

The book follows Hanna from modest beginnings in Småland to Norrtälje's seaside resort life, where she builds a guesthouse that becomes a family home, meeting place and social arena.

After a devastating family loss in 1885, Hanna's outlook changes. She becomes involved with the Salvation Army, works for women and children, and emerges as an early feminist, activist and female entrepreneur.

Based on Ernst Lindh's previously unpublished memoirs, archives, newspapers, photographs and new research.

The person behind it all

Hanna Lindh — the person behind it all

Early feministActivistEntrepreneurCommunity builder

Hanna Lindh (1839–1916) combined enterprise, care and social change.

After losing her third son in 1885, she began to see illness, poverty and class as part of the same reality. In the spring of 1886, she helped establish the Salvation Army in Norrtälje.

The guesthouse became more than a place for summer visitors. It became an arena for new ideas, social work and a woman who put action before words.

Hanna Lindh and Lindh's Guesthouse are an important part of the story of female entrepreneurship, the Salvation Army and Norrtälje's tourism history.

Pensionat Granparken

A guesthouse from 1879–1881

New research suggests that Pensionat Granparken was built between 1879 and 1881 and that the first guests moved in in 1883 — earlier than long assumed. These findings also add nuance to the image of Norrtälje as a purely fashionable resort town: it was also shaped by women’s enterprise, ideas of social reform and settings in which new ways of life could be explored.

About the author

Fredrik Anthony

Fredrik Anthony is an economist and art historian who grew up in Norrtälje.

For the past twenty years, he has worked with the valuation of art and antiques.

His previous publications include Nearly A Pyramide (2017), Not Street Art (2016), En akademisk homerun (2013) and Amphion (Swedish National Maritime Museum, 2007), among others.
